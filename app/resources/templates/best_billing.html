Best Billing Practices

Optimize your medical billing process with industry-leading best practices.

Maximize Your Revenue Cycle

Implementing best billing practices is essential for healthcare providers looking to optimize their revenue cycle management. Our comprehensive guide provides actionable insights to help you streamline your billing process, reduce denials, and accelerate payments.

At Ardur Healthcare, we've helped numerous practices transform their billing operations through proven methodologies and industry best practices. The strategies outlined below are based on our extensive experience and success in the healthcare billing industry.

Key Billing Best Practices

Implement these strategies to optimize your billing process.

Verify Insurance Eligibility

Always verify patient insurance eligibility before providing services. This simple step can significantly reduce claim denials and improve your clean claim rate.

  • Verify eligibility 2-3 days before scheduled appointments
  • Confirm coverage details, including copays and deductibles
  • Document verification details for future reference

Accurate Coding

Proper coding is the foundation of successful medical billing. Ensure your coding staff stays updated with the latest coding guidelines and regulations.

  • Use the most specific diagnosis codes available
  • Ensure proper linkage between procedure and diagnosis codes
  • Implement regular coding audits to identify areas for improvement

Timely Claim Submission

Submit claims as quickly as possible after service delivery to accelerate cash flow and avoid timely filing denials.

  • Aim to submit claims within 24-48 hours of service
  • Maintain a calendar of payer-specific filing deadlines
  • Implement a tracking system for claim submission status

Claim Scrubbing

Implement a robust claim scrubbing process to identify and correct errors before submission to payers.

  • Use automated claim scrubbing tools to identify potential issues
  • Review claims for common errors such as missing information or coding inconsistencies
  • Track and analyze rejection patterns to improve future submissions

Proactive Denial Management

Implement a systematic approach to identify, address, and prevent claim denials.

  • Analyze denial patterns to identify root causes
  • Develop specific action plans to address common denial reasons
  • Implement preventive measures to reduce future denials

Staff Training & Education

Invest in ongoing training and education for your billing staff to keep them updated with the latest industry changes.

  • Provide regular training on coding updates and payer policy changes
  • Encourage certification and continuing education
  • Conduct regular performance reviews and provide constructive feedback

Key Performance Indicators

Monitor these metrics to evaluate your billing performance.

Clean Claim Rate

Aim for a clean claim rate of 95% or higher. This indicates the percentage of claims that are accepted on first submission without rejection.

Days in A/R

Keep your days in accounts receivable under 30-40 days. This measures how quickly you're collecting payments after service delivery.

Denial Rate

Maintain a denial rate below 5%. This represents the percentage of claims denied by payers relative to total claims submitted.
