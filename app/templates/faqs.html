General Medical Billing

What is medical billing, and how does it work?

Medical billing is the process of submitting and following up on claims with health insurance companies to receive payment for services rendered. It involves charge entry, coding, claim submission, payment posting, and AR follow-up.

What’s the difference between medical billing and medical coding?

Medical billing involves submitting claims and handling payment processes, while medical coding is the translation of diagnoses and procedures into standardized codes (like CPT, ICD-10).

What are clean claims in medical billing?

Clean claims are those submitted without errors or omissions and are processed without the need for additional information.

What causes claim denials in medical billing?

Common causes include coding errors, eligibility issues, missing information, and lack of prior authorization.

How long does it take to get paid from insurance companies?

Most commercial insurers pay within 15–45 days of claim submission. Government payers like Medicare may take 10–30 days.

Credentialing and Enrollment

What is provider credentialing, and why is it important?

Credentialing is the process of verifying a provider’s qualifications with insurance networks. It's necessary for in-network participation and reimbursement.

How long does credentialing typically take?

It usually takes 60–90 days, depending on the payer and completeness of documentation.

Do you help with recredentialing or revalidation?

Yes, we manage all phases of credentialing: initial, recredentialing, and revalidation.

Can I see patients while my credentialing is in process?

You can, but you may not receive reimbursement until credentialing is complete. Some payers allow retroactive billing.
